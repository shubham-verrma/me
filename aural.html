The Story

Built on belief
before revenue.

"I started Aural at 19 with no money, no connections, and no clue what I was doing. What I had was a conviction that audio was the future for a billion Indians who couldn't read English fluently."
Social Audio Consumer Tech Next Billion Users Shark Tank India

It was 2022. I was sitting in my engineering hostel at Manipal University when the idea crystallised — India's next billion internet users weren't going to engage with text-heavy social media. They'd listen. They'd speak. They needed a platform built for audio-first communities in local languages and dialects.

That insight became Aural. I started with nothing but a pitch deck and an unshakeable belief. No salary. No funding. No office. I convinced 30+ people — designers, developers, marketers, content creators — to join me and build for free. That alone taught me more about leadership than any MBA ever could.

I learned to incorporate a company, file trademarks, navigate legal frameworks, build product roadmaps, pitch investors, and manage a cross-functional team — all before I graduated. The venture took us all the way to Shark Tank India Season 2, where we were selected from 200,000+ applicants to pitch directly to Sony's management team.

Aural ultimately paused operations in 2024. The consumer social market in India is brutally hard — monetisation, retention, and cold start problems compounded faster than we could solve them. But the company gave me something money can't buy: the operating system of a founder. Every lesson from Aural informs every engagement I take on today.

What I Learned

The curriculum
no college teaches.

01

Mission beats money — until it doesn't

You can attract incredible people with vision alone, but you can't sustain them. The transition from mission-driven to revenue-driven is where most consumer startups break. I learned it the hard way.

02

Cold start is the only real problem in consumer social

Every feature, every growth hack, every campaign is secondary to solving the cold start problem. Without liquidity and network effects in place from day one, the product never reaches escape velocity.

03

The pitch is never the product

Getting to Shark Tank taught me how to tell a compelling story. But I confused narrative momentum with product-market fit. The best founders I know are obsessed with the latter, not the former.

04

Operations kill more startups than bad ideas

Aural paused because of operational failures, not because the market didn't exist. Managing remote teams, cash flow, and delivery at zero salary is a full discipline on its own — and I wasn't prepared for it.
